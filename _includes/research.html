The MLSE Study

Development of a multilingual language assessment tool for assessing progressive aphasia

A study led by St George's Hospital, University of London, in conjunction with researchers at the University of Cambridge, and the University of Manchester. I am currently working as a Research Associate for the Cambridge arm of the MLSE study. The study aims to develop a brief linguistic assessment tool to classify progressive aphasic syndromes. I will also use the MLSE to characterise language impairment in other types of dementia.

NPH Research

Cognition and apathy in normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH)

This research was conducted as part of my PhD in the Department of Psychiatry at the University of Cambridge. I investigated cognitive outcome following shunt treatment for NPH, and structural correlates of cognitive impairment and apathy in NPH.
